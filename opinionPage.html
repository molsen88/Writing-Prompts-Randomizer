Opinion Writing

"The difference between opinions and convictions is this, men hold opinions, covictions hold men."-T.R. Slicer

Writing an Opinion Paper

When writing an opinion paper, it is important to stay on topic. Clearly define your opinion for the reader. The flow of your paper should allow the reader to easily connect your reasons to your opinion. Using text evidence from reliable sources also strengthens your opinion. Your writing should include an introduction with your opinion, a body section with your reasons and supporting details, and a clear conclusion.

  • Introduction Learn more!

    • Introductions in Opinion Writing


    What to include:

    • -An interesting and catching hook
    • -A clearly stated opinion
    • -Your main ideas (reasons to support your opinion)

    What is a hook?

    A hook is at the beginning of a paper to grab the reader's attention. You can use any of the following:

    • -Question(s)
    • -An interesting statistic or information
    • -An "imagine if..." or set a scene
    • -A personal story related to the topic

    Clearly stated opinion

    The most important sentence in your introduction is the sentence that cleary states your opinion.

    • -In my opinion....
    • -I think...
    • -I believe....
    • -I strongly agree that...

    Main Ideas

    The last sentence in your introduction should list your main ideas or reasons that support your opinion. After stating your opinion, you should let your reader know what reasons you will provide in the body section of your paper. You can restate the topic and follow it with your main ideas or reasons.

    • -Students should be recieve homework because it helps them remember lessons, it teaches responsibility, and it helps them practice.

    • -The city needs to replace roads because they are dangerous and they can cause damage to cars.

    Complete Example

    Did you know that 92% of schools don't allow homework to be given to students? Many schools have decided to abandon homework altogether citing various concerns from teachers and parents. In my opinion, students should be given homework. They should be given homework becuase it helps students practice concepts, it teaches students responsibility, and it helps parents to know what is being taught in school.

  • Body Paragraph(s) Learn more!

    • Body Paragraphs

    What should I include:

    Your body paragraphs should only focus on your main ideas and the supporting details. Your main ideas(reasons) should be in the same order as they were presented in the last sentence in your introduction paragraph.


    Signal Words:

    To easily transition between each main idea, you can use signal words to help your paper flow from one reason to another. The words or phrases you can use could be any of the following:

    • -My first, second, third reason
    • -Another reason...
    • -Next....
    • -Lastly...
    • -Finally...

    Organize:

    An author might choose to organize the information either by the two topics or by grouping the similarities and the differencs in to two sections.

  • Conclusion Learn more!

    • Sequence

    Purpose:

    To write about a person, place, thing, events, or ideas in time order or in an order that needs to be followed.

    Signal Words:

    first, second, third, beginning, after, next, later, lastly, another, dates references, time references, on, following, then, when, before

    Organize:

    An author would use sequence text structure to describe how a particular event took place, how make something in steps, or to write about someone's life. Writing in sequence text structure requires information to be grouped in the order it happened or needs to be done.
